Family Planning Methods

Natural, temporary (barrier, hormonal), long‑acting reversible (LARC), and permanent methods. Evidence‑based counseling, effectiveness, side effects, and nursing role.

Overview & Principles of Family Planning

Family planning enables individuals and couples to achieve desired number and spacing of children. Methods are classified as natural, temporary (artificial), long‑acting reversible (LARC), and permanent.
  • Contraceptive efficacy: Perfect use vs typical use. Perfect use = consistent/correct; typical use = real‑world (missed pills, etc.).
  • Continuation rate – proportion still using method after 1 year.
  • Dual protection: Use of condoms + another method to prevent both pregnancy and STIs.
  • LARC advantages: IUDs and implants have highest continuation and effectiveness (>99%).
  • Factors in method choice: Medical eligibility (WHO MEC), personal preference, side effects, cost, accessibility, future fertility desire.

Natural Family Planning (Fertility Awareness)

🌡️ Basal Body Temperature (BBT)
Temperature rise after ovulation indicates infertile phase. Requires daily morning temp. Perfect use 99%, typical 80%.
🌸 Cervical Mucus (Billings)
Observe mucus changes: wet, clear, stretchy (fertile) vs dry, thick (infertile). Perfect use 97%, typical 80%.
📅 Calendar / Rhythm
Calculate fertile window based on cycle length (usually days 8–19). Less reliable; perfect use 95%, typical 88%.
📱 Standard Days Method
CycleBeads for cycles 26–32 days; abstain days 8–19. Perfect use 95%, typical 88%.
🔁 Symptothermal Method
Combines BBT, mucus, and other signs. Highest efficacy among natural methods.
🤱 Lactational Amenorrhea Method (LAM)
Exclusive breastfeeding, <6 months, amenorrhea. Efficacy 98–99%. Temporary only.
⚠️ Natural methods require motivation, cycle regularity, and partner cooperation. No STI protection.

Temporary Methods (Barrier & Hormonal)

🛡️ Male Condom
Latex or polyurethane; dual protection (STI + pregnancy). Perfect 98%, typical 85%.
🛡️ Female Condom
Polyurethane sheath; less effective (21% failure typical).
💊 Combined Oral Contraceptive (COC)
Estrogen+progestin; inhibits ovulation, thickens mucus. Perfect 99.7%, typical 92%.
💊 Progestin‑Only Pill (POP)
No estrogen; safe during breastfeeding. Must take same time daily.
💉 DMPA (Depo‑Provera)
IM injection q12 weeks; amenorrhea common. Perfect 99.7%, typical 97%.
🌿 Vaginal Ring / Patch
Hormonal; weekly or monthly replacement. Efficacy similar to COC.
🍆 Spermicides & Diaphragm
Low efficacy alone; best with barrier. Nonoxynol‑9 can cause irritation.
⚠️ Estrogen‑containing methods contraindicated in smokers >35, uncontrolled hypertension, migraine with aura, history of thrombosis.

Long‑Acting Reversible (LARC) & Permanent Methods

🔗 Copper IUD (ParaGard)
Non‑hormonal, lasts 10–12 years. Efficacy >99%. Side effects: heavier bleeding, cramping.
💊 Levonorgestrel IUD (Mirena, Kyleena)
Hormonal, lasts 3–7 years. Lighter periods, may cause amenorrhea.
💊 Subdermal Implant (Nexplanon)
Single rod, lasts 3 years. Efficacy >99%. Irregular bleeding common.
✂️ Female Sterilization (Tubal Ligation)
Bilateral tubal occlusion. Permanent, >99% effective. Usually done laparoscopically or postpartum.
🔪 Male Sterilization (Vasectomy)
Cutting/blocking vas deferens. Permanent, 99.9% effective. Requires 3 months and semen analysis to confirm sterility.
✅ LARC has highest continuation rates and is recommended for adolescents and all women regardless of parity.

Nursing Role & Family Planning Counseling

  • BALANCED counseling model: Benefits, Alternatives, Limitations, Ability (correct use), Concerns, Expectations, Documentation.
  • Use GATHER approach: Greet, Ask, Tell, Help, Explain, Return.
  • Assess medical eligibility (WHO MEC categories 1–4).
  • Provide method‑specific teaching: How to use, side effects, warning signs, when to return.
  • Dual protection: Encourage condoms plus another method if STI risk.
  • Emergency contraception (EC): Copper IUD (most effective) or oral levonorgestrel (Plan B) up to 72–120 hours. Progestin‑only EC is OTC.
  • Postpartum contraception: Progestin‑only methods immediate; IUD/implant at 4–6 weeks; estrogen after 6 weeks if not breastfeeding.
